## Company Overview

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary Persona: "The Efficient Traveler"

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28-55
- Income: Upper-middle to high ($80K-250K+ annually)
- Occupation: Business professionals, consultants, remote workers, entrepreneurs, frequent leisure travelers
- Location: Australia, Singapore, UK, Malaysia, Indonesia (our core markets)
- Travel frequency: 3-12+ international trips per year

**Psychographics:**
- Values time over money‚Äîwilling to pay more for convenience
- Tech-comfortable but not necessarily tech-obsessed
- Expects premium service and reliability
- Dislikes complexity, friction, and hidden fees
- Often travels for both business and leisure
- Makes decisions quickly when value is clear

**Pain Points:**
1. **Roaming shock** - Received massive bills from carriers like Telstra, Singtel, Vodafone after trips
2. **Airport SIM hassle** - Wasted time finding SIM vendors, filling forms, waiting in queues
3. **Connectivity anxiety** - Fear of being unreachable for work or family while abroad
4. **Plan confusion** - Overwhelmed by carrier roaming packages with hidden terms
5. **Currency confusion** - Frustration with USD pricing when they think in AUD/SGD/GBP

**Buying Triggers:**
- Upcoming international trip (1-14 days before departure)
- Bad experience with roaming charges on previous trip
- Recommendation from trusted colleague or friend
- Seeing comparison of roaming costs vs eSIM

**Decision Criteria (in order):**
1. Reliability - Will it actually work when I land?
2. Simplicity - Can I set this up in 5 minutes?
3. Value - Am I saving money vs roaming?
4. Support - What if something goes wrong?
5. Flexibility - Can I top up if I need more data?

### Secondary Persona: "The Digital Nomad"

- Longer trips (1-6 months)
- Needs reliable data for work
- More price-conscious due to extended travel
- Values flexibility and regional plans
- Often recommends to community

### Anti-Persona (Who We're NOT For):

- Budget backpackers seeking cheapest option at any cost
- People who rarely travel internationally
- Those who prefer physical SIM cards "just in case"
- Users who need extensive voice minutes (we're data-focused)

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Attributes

1. **Confident, Not Arrogant**
   - We know our product works. We state facts, not hyperbole.
   - ‚úÖ "Connect in under 3 minutes"
   - ‚ùå "The BEST eSIM in the world!!!"

2. **Clear, Not Dumbed Down**
   - Premium customers are intelligent. Respect that.
   - ‚úÖ "Your eSIM activates automatically when you land"
   - ‚ùå "Don't worry, it's super easy even if you're not techy!"

3. **Warm, Not Overly Casual**
   - Professional but human. Like a trusted travel advisor.
   - ‚úÖ "We're here if you need us‚Äîreal humans on WhatsApp"
   - ‚ùå "Hit us up anytime fam! üî•"

4. **Direct, Not Pushy**
   - State the value. Let customers decide.
   - ‚úÖ "See how much you'll save vs Telstra roaming"
   - ‚ùå "BUY NOW before it's too late!!!"

### Tone Spectrum by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Homepage | Confident, welcoming | "Travel light. Stay connected." |
| Product pages | Clear, informative | "15GB of high-speed data across Japan. Valid for 30 days." |
| Checkout | Reassuring, minimal | "Secure checkout. Your eSIM will be ready instantly." |
| Support | Warm, helpful | "Let's get you connected. What's happening?" |
| Error states | Calm, solution-focused | "Something went wrong. Let's try that again." |
| Blog | Authoritative, helpful | "Here's exactly what you need to know about Japan's eSIM coverage" |

### Language Principles

**DO:**
- Use active voice
- Lead with benefits, follow with features
- Be specific with numbers ("3 minutes" not "quick")
- Acknowledge the pain point before presenting solution
- Use "you/your" more than "we/our"
- Write at 8th-grade reading level for clarity
- Use local currency and local comparisons

**DON'T:**
- Use jargon (LTE bands, APN settings) unless necessary
- Make promises we can't keep
- Use fear tactics or false urgency
- Sound like every other eSIM company
- Use emojis in primary copy (sparingly OK in support chat)
- Say "cheap" (use "affordable" or focus on value)

## Brand Differentiators

### What Makes Trvel Different

1. **Local Currency Pricing**
   - We price in YOUR currency (AUD, SGD, GBP, MYR, IDR)
   - No hidden conversion fees or PayPal surcharges
   - Price you see = price you pay

2. **Local Carrier Comparison**
   - We show you exactly what Telstra/Singtel/EE would charge
   - Real savings made tangible, not vague claims
   - "Save $147 vs Telstra roaming on this trip"

3. **Premium Reliability**
   - We use tier-1 local carriers in each destination
   - 10-minute connection guarantee or full refund
   - No throttling on "unlimited" plans

4. **Human Support**
   - Real people on WhatsApp (not chatbots)
   - Response in minutes, not days
   - We speak your language

5. **Instant Delivery**
   - eSIM delivered to email in seconds
   - Set up before you leave home
   - No airport queues, no physical cards
